Christina Harvey is an engineer and zoologist who seeks to understand the fundamental principles supporting how birds fly. Her research combines biomechanics, aerodynamics, flight dynamics, and animal behavior to uncover biological flight principles and determine which of those principles can benefit in engineering designs.

Her work bridges engineering and biology, with a BEng in Mechanical Engineering (McGill University), an MSc in Zoology (University of British Columbia), and a PhD in Aerospace Engineering (University of Michigan). That perspective shapes the BIRD Lab, where engineers and biologists work together to answer questions neither discipline could solve alone.

Christina is a 2021 Amelia Earhart Fellow, a 2023 Packard Fellow and leads the Center for Animal Locomotion & Innovation (CALI). She is passionate about rigorous science, thoughtful mentoring, and building an open, collaborative research environment where curiosity comes first.
