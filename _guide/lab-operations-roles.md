**Purpose.** Keep the lab running smoothly with clear, lightweight ownership of
essential operational tasks — so safety, facilities, equipment, data, and
onboarding don't fall through the cracks, and we can stay focused on science.

**Cadence.** Weekly (2–5 min): owners review assigned tasks in the task tracker.
Monthly (10–15 min): owners update checklists and flag overdue items. Quarterly
(30–60 min): owners complete reviews, walkthroughs, and compliance checks.
Annually (4–6 hours): owners propose process improvements. Most roles average
~10–30 min/week.

**Escalation.** If something is blocked for over a week — or immediately if it's
safety- or compliance-critical — the owner reaches out to Christina with *what
happened, what you need, and by when.*

<div class="callout callout--warn" markdown="1">
No role should require chasing individuals. If someone doesn't comply twice, or
you find yourself chasing external folks, escalate to Christina.
</div>

The roles below rotate among lab members. Each owner maintains a handoff page so
the next person can run the role from that page alone.

## The roles

**Onboarding Officer** — makes onboarding fast, consistent, and safe: onboarding
checklists, initial-training coordination, access-request guidance, and starter
templates. Can require completion of the onboarding checklist before independent
work.

**Safety Officer** — keeps safety practices current and visible: walkthroughs,
certification tracking, documentation, and critical-equipment checks. Can pause
unsafe work and require refresher training before re-access.

**Facilities & Infrastructure Coordinator** — keeps spaces functional and reduces
downtime: facilities tickets, infrastructure issues, and room readiness. Can
submit facilities requests and set limited-access windows for repairs.

**Equipment & Experiment Coordinator** — keeps core equipment reliable and easy to
use: maintenance schedules, calibration reminders, and SOP/lead-user coverage for
each major system (wind tunnel, cameras, 3D printer). Can restrict equipment use
until training/SOP requirements are met.

**IT & Data Administrator** — keeps code and data organized, backed up, and
reproducible: GitHub norms, data-storage structure, backups, permissions, and
license tracking. Can require a minimum repo/data structure and lock down
permissions if integrity is at risk.

**Communication & Outreach Lead** — keeps the lab's outward-facing presence
legible and attractive to collaborators, recruits, and funders: website updates,
social media, tour scripts, and outreach events. Can publish routine updates and
set a monthly content schedule.

**Animal Care Coordinator** — ensures animal welfare and compliance are handled
predictably: feeding/monitoring schedules, welfare logs, IACUC reminders, and
Raptor Center coordination. Can pause animal-related activities if welfare or
compliance is at risk.

**Inventory & Process Improvement Lead** — keeps common consumables stocked and
launches small workflow upgrades: inventory tracking, reorder thresholds, and the
annual chemical-inventory review. Can reorder consumables under an agreed
threshold.

**Offboarding & Access Control Officer** — makes departures smooth and low-risk:
offboarding checklists, access removal, ownership transfer, and the quarterly
access audit. Can freeze or remove access to lab systems after a member's last
day.
