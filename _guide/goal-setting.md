We plan at two horizons: a long-term **Individual Development Plan** that names
where you're headed, and **quarterly goals** that move you there in concrete
steps. We review both in one-on-one meetings, and each one shapes the other.

## Your Individual Development Plan (IDP)

An IDP is a living document that maps where you want to end up to the skills and
experiences that will get you there. Revisit it at least once a year, and let it
guide what you take on each quarter.

Reflect on, and bring to your first one-on-one:

- **Direction:** your long-term goals and likely path (academia, industry, or
  government). See [Career planning & job search]({{ '/lab-guide/career-planning/' | relative_url }}).
- **Skills to build:** technical and non-technical, and how you'll practice them.
- **Experiences you want:** teaching, mentoring, conferences, collaborations,
  internships.
- **Steps and timeline:** the milestones between here and there, roughly when.

You don't need finished answers. The IDP is a starting point you refine as you
learn what fits you.

## Set quarterly SMART goals

Each quarter, turn part of your plan into goals you can actually check off.

**1. Rough goal (brain dump).** Write it in natural language, without worrying
yet whether it's "SMART."

**2. SMART details.**

- **Specific.** What exactly will be done or produced?
- **Measurable.** How will you know it's done or progressing (experiments, figures, pages, submissions)?
- **Actionable.** What are the key actions you'll take, and what depends on others?
- **Realistic.** Is this achievable this quarter given your time and constraints? What will you *not* do?
- **Time-bound.** Final deadline (end of quarter) plus early, mid, and late checkpoints.

**3. Challenges, resources, and minimum success.**

- **Likely challenges** (time, approvals, methods, data).
- **Resources / skills needed** (people, equipment, analysis skills, training).
- **Minimum viable success** (a smaller version that still feels like a win).

**4. Final SMART goal (1–3 sentences).** Rewrite it so someone outside your
project would understand it.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
**Template:** *"By [date], I will [produce X] by [doing Y], tracked by [measure],
with [minimum viable goal / backup plan] if [likely challenge] occurs."*
</div>

## Meet your goals

A goal only counts once it's done. Close the gap between plan and finish:

- **Break it into action steps,** each with an owner (you or someone else), a
  target date, and a done box.
- **Make progress visible.** A Kanban board or simple timeline keeps the work in
  front of you and surfaces stalls early.
- **Review in your goals** and adjust when reality shifts. A missed goal is
  information, not failure. Note any issues, and carry the lesson into next quarter.
