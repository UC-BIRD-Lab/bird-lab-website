We set goals every quarter and review them in one-on-one meetings. Work through
the steps below.

## 1. Rough goal (brain dump)

Write your goal in natural language, without worrying yet whether it's "SMART."

## 2. SMART details

- **Specific** — what exactly will be done or produced?
- **Measurable** — how will you know it's done or progressing (experiments, figures, pages, submissions)?
- **Actionable** — what are the key actions you'll take, and what depends on others?
- **Realistic** — is this achievable this quarter given your time and constraints? What will you *not* do?
- **Time-bound** — final deadline (end of quarter) plus early, mid, and late checkpoints.

## 3. Challenges, resources, and minimum success

- **Likely challenges** (time, approvals, methods, data).
- **Resources / skills needed** (people, equipment, analysis skills, training).
- **Minimum viable success** — a smaller version that still feels like a win.

## 4. Final SMART goal (1–3 sentences)

Rewrite your goal so someone outside your project would understand it.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
**Template:** *"By [date], I will [produce X] by [doing Y], tracked by [measure],
with [minimum viable goal / backup plan] if [likely challenge] occurs."*
</div>

## 5. Action-step checklist

Turn the goal into concrete steps, each with an owner (you or someone else), a
target date, and a done box.
