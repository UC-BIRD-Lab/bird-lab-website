## Research & growth <span class="when">Ongoing</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Mentor students.** You help mentor the lab's undergraduates. Support their
onboarding and day-to-day questions, and model good practice; see
[Mentoring]({{ '/lab-guide/mentoring/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**2. Plan your development and next step.** Set an individual development plan and
map your career direction; see [Goal setting]({{ '/lab-guide/goal-setting/' | relative_url }})
and [Career planning & job search]({{ '/lab-guide/career-planning/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**3. Apply for fellowships and grants.** Postdoctoral fellowships and grants build
toward your independence; see [Funding applications]({{ '/lab-guide/funding-applications/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**4. Complete university onboarding.** Enroll in benefits following instructions in UC Davis HR onboarding. International scholars may also need to work with [Services for
International Students and Scholars](https://siss.ucdavis.edu).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**5. Prepare your year-end review.** Once a year, reflect on your progress and draft
a short year-end review before meeting with Christina. Cover:

- Research contributions: publications, protocols and SOPs, and technical skills gained.
- Project planning and problem-solving: managing timelines and handling challenges.
- Mentorship and training: helping or training others, and any sessions you led.
- Research impact and visibility: presentations, outreach, and new research scope.
- Professional development: growth areas, new ideas proposed, and funding you explored.
- Overall progress and where you want to grow next.
{: .checklist}

Draft your reflections first; Christina uses them to focus the review conversation on your development and next step.
{: .guide-info}
</div>
