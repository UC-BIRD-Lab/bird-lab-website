Welcome to the BIRD Lab at UC Davis! 🎉 Use this page as a checklist during your
first year. Most items are "do once," some are ongoing.

## Section A · Orientation & access (Week 1)

**1. Read the [Lab Guide]({{ '/lab-guide/' | relative_url }}) in full.**

**2. Complete safety training and gain access.**

- Complete all initial trainings in [Be Safe]({{ '/lab-guide/be-safe/' | relative_url }}).
- Complete the Key Authorization Form to get an office key.
- Request the door code to the Wind Tunnel Building (office: WTB 1401).

**3. Install core tools and set up data backups.**

- Set up [UC Davis Microsoft 365](https://iet.ucdavis.edu/content/free-microsoft-office-365-now-available-all-uc-davis-students), a synced Google Calendar, and the Box Desktop App.
- Request an external hard drive (email Christina).

<div class="callout callout--warn" markdown="1">
All research documents must be backed up in three places: your workstation
(daily), Box (daily), and your hard drive (monthly). All code must be
version-controlled with Git.
</div>

- Install [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/) + its [Word plugin](https://www.zotero.org/support/word_processor_plugin_usage).
- Install [Git](https://github.com/git-guides/install-git), create a GitHub account, and (if new) complete [Introduction to GitHub](https://lab.github.com/githubtraining/introduction-to-github).
- Set up [R & RStudio](https://rstudio-education.github.io/hopr/starting.html) and [Python & VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/).

**4. Request access and schedule meetings.** Email Christina for Slack, a team-page
entry, Box access, and GitHub-organization access (with your username). Get added
to the bi-weekly lab meeting and a weekly 30-minute 1:1. Christina sends a
scheduling poll at the start of each quarter — see [Meetings]({{ '/lab-guide/meetings/' | relative_url }}).

## Section B · Identity, goals & mentoring (Weeks 1–2)

**1. Reflect on your goals** (career, professional development, research, skills,
and the steps to get there) and bring your answers to your first 1:1.

**2. Create your research profiles** (use your UC Davis email during your degree):
[ORCID](https://orcid.org), [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com),
[ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net), [FigShare](https://figshare.com).

**3. Draft your quarterly [SMART goals]({{ '/lab-guide/develop-smart-goals/' | relative_url }})** and review them in your 1:1s.

## Section C · Foundation year (Year 1)

**1. Begin your literature review.** This is your core intellectual foundation —
see [Review the literature]({{ '/lab-guide/review-the-literature/' | relative_url }}).
Start a systematic review, organize it in Zotero, and build a living library you
update throughout the year.

**2. Create a long-term plan.** By the end of your first year, build a Gantt chart
or visual timeline (coursework, quals/FE, research milestones, paper submissions,
conferences, thesis goals) and refine it in your 1:1s. A Kanban board is
recommended.

**3. Select your course schedule.** Courses should support your research and skill
growth. Draft a plan aligned with your project and timeline and discuss it with
Christina. BIRD Lab expectations include at least one statistics course (e.g.,
EVE 225, or MAE 298 with Dr. Whittle) and a bird course (e.g., WFC 111).

**4. Pass the preliminary exam (or FE).** PhD students are expected to pass in
their first year.
