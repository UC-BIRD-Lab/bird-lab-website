Be on time to all meetings, and give at least 24 hours' notice if you can't
attend.

## One-on-one meetings

<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
New to the lab? Setup for the weekly check-in is in the
[member portal]({{ '/portal/' | relative_url }}).
</aside>

- All graduate students and postdocs have weekly 30-minute one-on-ones. (Mentoring someone yourself? See [Mentoring]({{ '/lab-guide/mentoring/' | relative_url }}).)
- Submit the **Weekly Check-In** Slack workflow by **7:00am** on the day of your 1:1. It takes about three to five minutes and covers five things:
  - **Accomplishments and learnings:** what progress or learning happened this week.
  - **Blockers and challenges:** what slowed you down, and what help would unblock you.
  - **Next-week priorities:** your top priorities, plus the first smallest next step.
  - **Next deadline:** the exact date(s), whether you are on track, and what would make it a "yes."
  - **Requests for Christina:** questions, decisions, feedback, introductions, resources, and so on.
- Need more than 30 minutes, or a one-off meeting? Arrange it with Christina over email or Slack in advance.
- Did course work or other life events get in the way this week? Not an issue! Let Christina know; we will cancel and pick back up next week. Please still submit your Slack check-in.

Once a year there is also a longer progress review, in addition to the weekly meetings: graduate students complete the UC Davis [Student Progress Assessment](https://grad.ucdavis.edu/student-progress-assessment) with focused one-hour one-on-ones in June, and postdocs prepare a year-end review (see the [graduate student]({{ '/lab-guide/graduate-students/' | relative_url }}) and [postdoc]({{ '/lab-guide/postdocs/' | relative_url }}) start guides).

## Lab meetings

<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
Volunteers aren't required to attend; regular attendance is one way we see engagement, but it is not the only way.
</aside>

<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
When it's your turn to present don't forget to review the lab [presentation suggestions]({{ '/lab-guide/presentations/' | relative_url }}).
</aside>

- During the academic year, we meet every week with a mix of research updates, lab upkeep meetings, and professional development.
- During the summer quarter, we meet every week but we alternate between research updates and journal club.
- All lab members are expected to attend.
- Graduate students and postdoctoral scholars present their research roughly **twice a year**.
- Present both your results and successes *and* the challenges you faced getting there. Sharing what went wrong helps destigmatize failure, a necessary practice in research, and often leads to better ideas from the room.
- Give presenters your attention and follow our
  [discussion guidelines]({{ '/lab-guide/discussion-guidelines/' | relative_url }}).

## Journal club

We meet every two weeks to discuss a new article. See [Journal club]({{ '/lab-guide/journal-club/' | relative_url }}) for the format and discussion norms.
