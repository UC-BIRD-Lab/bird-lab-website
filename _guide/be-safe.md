The safety of every member of our team and community is of the utmost
importance. We take our safety responsibilities seriously and commit to creating
and upholding a safe lab culture.

<div class="callout callout--stop" markdown="1">
If you see anything that seems unsafe — or even slightly raises a question —
ask and act immediately rather than waiting. Raise any concern directly to
Christina. Suggestions that make the lab safer are always appreciated.
</div>

## Member rights and obligations

**You have the right to** know about hazards in your lab space, obtain
hazard-specific training, and work in a properly designed, equipped, and
maintained facility.

**You are responsible for** participating in hazard-identification training,
following UC Davis and BIRD Lab safety policies, correcting unsafe acts within
your ability, and reporting any unsafe situation without fear of retaliation.

## Minimum personal protective equipment (PPE)

- Closed-toe shoes (no crocs).
- Full-length pants.

Additional equipment may be required depending on what you're using. No food or
drink is allowed in the main wind tunnel room (Room 1402).

## Required training (all members, before working in the office)

1. Sign up for [MAE annual safety training](https://mae.ucdavis.edu/events) at the earliest date and get the certificate of completion.
2. Complete the relevant LMS modules and keep the certificates:
   - [Hazard Communication](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/training/hazard-communication) *(everyone)*
   - PPE: Personal Protective Equipment *(everyone; covered if you've done UC Laboratory Safety Fundamentals)*
   - [Hand and Power Tool Safety](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/training/hand-and-power-tool-safety) *(experimental work)*
   - Ladder Safety *(anyone using ladders)*
   - [Electrical Safety](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/training/electrical-safety) *(wind-tunnel operators)*
3. Review all relevant Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for what you'll use.
4. Complete the "Initial Safety Training — Office and WT" workflow in Slack, including site-specific training with your assigned trainer.

**Recurring:** complete the annual refresher (each fall, during a lab meeting)
and the MAE Annual Safety Orientation; send proof to the lab manager.

## Facility-specific training

**Wind tunnel operation.** Review the wind-tunnel safety section of the
Experimenters Safety Document and complete a walkthrough with the PI.

**Working with live animals at CALI** 🦅

<div class="callout callout--warn" markdown="1">
Raptors are essentially flying knives. A high standard of safety in this facility
is critical — for the birds and for people.
</div>

1. Complete the ACU 101 course if you'll work with live animals (re-take the [ACU 101 refresher](https://research.ucdavis.edu/policiescompliance/animal-care-use/training-classes/animal-care-and-use-101-retraining-quiz/) every 3 years).
2. Read [Care and Use of Birds, Owls and Raptors](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/units/occupational-health/surveillance-system/zoonotic-diseases/birds) (includes zoonotic-disease review).
3. Review [Animal Bites and Scratches](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/units/occupational-health/surveillance-system/animal-bites) procedures and the reporting process.
4. Review [Animal Allergies](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/units/occupational-health/surveillance-system/animal-allergies) information.
5. Complete all California Raptor Center requirements (ask Christina for current guidelines).
6. Complete the "Initial Safety Training — CALI" workflow in Slack, including the OHSS Risk Assessment and Health Surveillance Questionnaire.

Any concern about animal care or welfare should be reported directly; see the
[IACUC policy on concern reporting](https://research.ucdavis.edu/wp-content/uploads/IACUC-14.pdf).

**Motion-capture cameras** 📸 — read the Optitrack methodology document and work
through the Flex 13 system with a graduate student before running experiments.

**Avian specimen handling** — sign the Avian Specimen Handling SOP and complete an
introductory walkthrough with a graduate student.

## Lab inspections

Monthly: emergency-eyewash test and fire-extinguisher visual checks. Every six
months: a lab cleaning day with the inspection checklist and self-inspection.
Annually: an internal EH&S General and Occupational Safety inspection.

## Documentation

The Injury and Illness Prevention Plan (IIPP) and Emergency Action Plan (EAP) are
maintained through the MAE Department ([Safety](https://mae.ucdavis.edu/safety)).
Report injuries via [UC Davis injury reporting](https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/units/risk-management-services/workers-compensation/injury-reporting).
