The lab prepares you for research careers in academia, industry, and government alike, and views all three as equally worthy.

## Start early

- **Talk about it openly.** Bring career questions to your 1:1s. Christina will help you weigh paths honestly, including ones outside academia.
- **Fold it into your goals.** Use your quarterly
  [goals]({{ '/lab-guide/goal-setting/' | relative_url }}) to build the skills and outputs your target path rewards.
- **Use UC Davis resources.** The
  [GradPathways Institute](https://gradpathways.ucdavis.edu/) runs workshops on career readiness, and its
  [Career Management](https://gradpathways.ucdavis.edu/career-management) program supports both academic and non-academic paths.

## Know the paths

- **Academia**: postdoc, then faculty or research-scientist roles. Hiring runs on a yearly cycle (most faculty searches open in the fall) and weighs publications, funding, teaching, and fit.
- **Industry**: R&D, engineering, and data roles. Hiring is rolling and
  faster-moving, and values demonstrated skills, portfolios, and the ability to
  explain your work to non-specialists.
- **Government & national labs**: research and technical roles that blend
  elements of both, often with longer timelines and formal application steps.

## Build your materials

Keep these current as you go, not just when you apply (see
[Professional presence]({{ '/lab-guide/professional-presence/' | relative_url }})).

- **CV** for academic and research roles; a one-to-two-page **résumé** tailored to
  each industry role.
- **Statements** as needed: research, teaching, and diversity statements for
  faculty applications; a **cover letter** tailored to each posting.
- **A portfolio or work samples** (papers, code, figures, or a talk) that show
  what you can do.

## Run the search

- **Network intentionally.** Conferences are one of the best places to meet future
  colleagues and employers; see
  [Conferences]({{ '/lab-guide/conferences/' | relative_url }}).
  Informational conversations often matter more than job boards.
- **Request letters early.** Give recommenders plenty of lead time and the context
  they need; see
  [Recommendation letters]({{ '/lab-guide/recommendation-letters/' | relative_url }}).
- **Tailor every application** to the specific role, and have someone in the lab
  read your materials before you submit.
