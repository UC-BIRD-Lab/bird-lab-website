These guidelines exist to support productive, inclusive discussions and a brave
space. We expect all members to follow them in every discussion — technical or
personal. We review them as a lab once a year so new and continuing members
share the same expectations.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
A **brave space** is one where participants feel comfortable learning, sharing,
and growing — inclusive across race, sex, gender, ability, immigration status,
and lived experience. Everyone acknowledges there may be discomfort when
discussing difficult topics, and honors each other's experiences and opinions
with respect to reach understanding.
</div>

<div class="callout callout--stop" markdown="1">
We **do not tolerate harassment of any kind**, no exceptions. If something makes
you uncomfortable, reach out to Christina (a Responsible Employee) or use the
confidential and non-confidential options on the
[Support resources]({{ '/lab-guide/support-resources/' | relative_url }}) page.
</div>

When others are presenting or during meetings, please use
[active listening](https://www.verywellmind.com/what-is-active-listening-3024343)
and set aside laptops and phones.

## In all interactions, we ask that you…

**Recognize…**

- that none of us are experts on everything — everyone is here to learn.
- that this is a professional environment; respect professional boundaries.
- the difference between opinion and informed knowledge backed by research; both
  can be valid to share, but lean toward informed knowledge and thoughtful reflection.
- that people have different needs — give others time to think and space to process.
  If you're a chatty person, make a conscious effort to leave room for quieter voices.
- that we must work to overcome historical and divisive biases such as racism and
  sexism; this takes intentional thought.
- that no one should be expected to speak for a whole demographic.

**Acknowledge…**

- your own social positionality and how it shapes your perspective.
- that two valid positions can be mutually exclusive — seek to understand before judging.
- that we're all taught misinformation about our own and other groups.
- that we may be at different stages of learning — be empathetic.

**Respect…**

- the speaker — one person speaks at a time.
- others' experiences — use "I" statements; don't speak for others without consent;
  don't play devil's advocate.
- confidentiality — "share a message, not the messenger."

**Don't blame…**

- yourself or others for mistakes — recognize, acknowledge, and move forward.
- yourself or others for misinformation learned, but take responsibility for not
  repeating it once you know better.

**Trust…**

- that people are doing their best; everyone has bad days.
- that you can ask for help — it's okay not to know.
- that you can be honest about your feelings and opinions.

**Take responsibility…**

- for actively listening and staying engaged.
- for including all voices — step up if you contribute too little, step back if too much.
- for addressing ideas, not people.
- for your own mistakes and defensive reactions, using them as entry points for
  deeper self-knowledge.
