This page offers suggestions for reviewing a paper in your field critically and
constructively. Adhere to all ethical guidelines, and **do not share the contents
of a paper under review with others**.

1. Read through the journal's review policies.
2. Read the paper in full.
3. Write a 2–3 sentence summary of the paper's main goals and findings.
4. Wait one week. ⏳
5. Re-read the paper, noting key issues as you go.
6. Sort the issues into three categories:
   - **Major issues** — things that must be fixed before publication (errors, significant oversights).
   - **Minor items** — things the authors can fix easily, or that aren't critical but would improve the work.
   - **Specific line items** — typos, confusing language, or sentences that contribute to a major/minor issue.
7. Re-read your review and verify the language is supportive, assumes nothing about the authors, and aims to improve the science.
