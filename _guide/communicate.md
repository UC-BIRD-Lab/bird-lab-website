## Communicating with Christina

**Expectations**

- During the work day, please respond to emails in a timely manner. Christina
  answers email quickly and appreciates the same in return — but recognizes
  others work differently, so prioritize replying when you can.
- Avoid non-urgent emails or Slack messages outside the work day or on weekends
  unless agreed in advance. Christina does the same (she often schedules messages
  to send later).
- Share your specific communication needs.
- CC Christina when reaching out to people outside the lab for the first time.
- Grad students: send your course schedule to Christina before registering.

<div class="callout callout--stop" markdown="1">
In an **emergency**, call Christina directly. If she doesn't pick up, send a text
with the details. (Members have her number; contact info is in the
[member portal]({{ '/portal/' | relative_url }}).)
</div>

**Picking the right channel**

- **Quick reply:** Slack, for urgent or short items.
- **Longer reply:** email. If you're requesting several things, use a numbered
  list; flag deadlines with date and time. Christina acknowledges email within
  1–2 days with an action plan — follow-ups are always welcome.
- **A meeting:** stop by the office, or send a [when2meet](https://www.when2meet.com)
  with your requested times and whether it's a 30- or 60-minute meeting.
- **Documents:** please share over email.

## Communicating with other lab members

Ask others which methods they prefer, and use topic-specific Slack channels for
discussions.

- All official lab communication should stay professional.
- Members are expected to resolve conflict among themselves, looping in Christina
  if further mediation is needed.
- Undergraduates are always welcome to bring mentorship issues to Christina.

## Giving and receiving feedback

Feedback — especially constructive feedback — can be uncomfortable, but it's a
vital part of research.

**Giving feedback**

- Make sure the recipient is ready for it; if unsure, ask.
- Balance warm and cool feedback — name strengths as well as opportunities.
- Focus on the work, not the person. Be specific; avoid vague "nice job!".
- Be timely, but take time to plan primarily-cool feedback.
- Follow up — feedback is an ongoing process, and note positive changes you see.

**Receiving feedback**

- Listen attentively and make sure you understand it, even if you disagree.
- Ask clarifying questions.
- Reflect — you're not obligated to follow all feedback (unless Christina says
  so); consider a second opinion if you disagree.
- Follow up to get multiple rounds where possible.

## Resolving conflict

Keep Christina in the loop if a problem feels overwhelming or unresolved. Tips
for constructive resolution:

- **Raise issues early** — silence can lead to escalation or resentment.
- **Define the conflict** — is it structural, a communication breakdown, a clash
  of expectations?
- **Manage your emotions first** — collect your thoughts before confronting an issue.
- **Acknowledge your part** in creating the conflict.
- **Listen actively** — paraphrase, give everyone space to be heard.
- **Ask for help** — a neutral third party or mediator can keep things flowing.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
**"I" statements.** A useful frame:
*"I feel ___ when (you) ___ because ___. What I'm hoping we might try is ___."*
Identify your feelings, their cause, the context for *why*, and the need or ideal
you'd like to work toward — adapted to your own voice and culture.
</div>
