The lab is an equal-opportunity employer. All opportunities are posted publicly
so any qualified person can apply.

## Work hours

- **Graduate students** are asked to maintain at least a 40-hour work week,
  including coursework and research. Early on, more time goes to classes; that
  balance shifts over time.
- **Undergraduate students** are asked to commit about 10 hours per week. This
  can be sporadic, as these are often volunteer positions. Keep your grades up,
  and let Christina know if you need to adjust in a given week.
- **Flexible hours are supported for everyone.** There are no fixed expectations
  about when or how long you are in the office, as long as the work gets done and
  your project is making good progress.
- Lab members are **not** expected to work weekends.
- If you are unwell — emotionally or physically — please take care of yourself.
  Illness is not a personal failing.
- See [Time off & vacation]({{ '/lab-guide/vacation/' | relative_url }}) for leave policies.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
If you're unsure whether you're working enough, too much, or on the right
things, try a **time audit** for 2–3 weeks. Tools like
[Toggl Track](https://toggl.com/track/) can help.
</div>

## Compensation

- PhD students are paid. MSc students are not guaranteed payment, but it is
  prioritized in the budget.
- If funds are available, undergraduates may be paid after a one-quarter trial
  period, provided they can dedicate the time and meet expectations set with
  their supervisor.

## Project management

- Each student is expected to manage their own project — setting and meeting
  targets — with support to build those skills.
- We use Gantt charts, Kanban boards, and similar tools to take an intentional
  approach to research project management.

## Deadlines

- Set and respect internal deadlines to avoid last-minute crunches.
- Give as much advance notice as possible. Christina aims to finish tasks at
  least a week before deadlines; with enough lead time, she will do her best to
  avoid working right up to the wire.

<div class="callout callout--warn" markdown="1">
If there are substantial differences between our working styles, we'll discuss
them directly in a one-on-one to set an intentional plan for working together
effectively.
</div>
