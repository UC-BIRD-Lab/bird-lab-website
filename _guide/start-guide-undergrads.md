Welcome to the BIRD Lab at UC Davis! 🎉 Use this page as a checklist during your
first quarter.

## At a glance

- **Typical time commitment:** ~8–10 hours/week (varies by quarter and project).
- **What you'll typically do:** help with data collection/processing, basic
  analyses, and/or literature review under the guidance of a grad student or postdoc.
- **What you can expect from us:** a defined mentor, regular 1:1 check-ins, and
  support in learning tools and research skills.
- **What we expect from you:** show up reliably, communicate clearly, ask
  questions, and keep your work organized and backed up.

## Section A · Orientation & expectations (Week 1)

**1. Read through lab info.** Read the [Lab Guide]({{ '/lab-guide/' | relative_url }})
and skim the other pages to get a sense of what's where.

**2. Complete safety training and gain access.**

- Complete all initial trainings in [Be Safe]({{ '/lab-guide/be-safe/' | relative_url }}).
- *(If you're not currently enrolled)* fill out the Volunteer Form and return it to Christina for signature.
- Request the door code to the Wind Tunnel Building (ask your mentor or the onboarding officer).

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Undergrads are not given office keys and are not to be in the facilities alone.
</div>

**3. Install core tools and set up data backups.**

- Set up your [UC Davis Microsoft 365](https://iet.ucdavis.edu/content/free-microsoft-office-365-now-available-all-uc-davis-students).
- Set up a Google Calendar synced to your phone and desktop.
- Install the Box Desktop App and create a folder system that syncs to Box.

<div class="callout callout--warn" markdown="1">
All research documents must be backed up in at least two places at all times —
your workstation and Box (daily). All code must be version-controlled with Git.
</div>

Recommended (ask your mentor which you'll use):

- [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/) reference manager + its [Word plugin](https://www.zotero.org/support/word_processor_plugin_usage).
- [Git](https://github.com/git-guides/install-git) and a GitHub account; if new, do [Introduction to GitHub](https://lab.github.com/githubtraining/introduction-to-github).
- Programming environment for your project: [R & RStudio](https://rstudio-education.github.io/hopr/starting.html) and/or [Python & VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/).

**4. Request access and schedule meetings.**

- Set a weekly 30-minute one-on-one with your mentor (see [Meetings]({{ '/lab-guide/meetings/' | relative_url }})).
- Email Christina (from your UC Davis address) to request Slack access and an entry on the team page.
- Email the lab's Box and GitHub owners to request access to lab folders and the GitHub organization (include your GitHub username).

## Section B · Identity, goals & mentoring (Weeks 1–2)

**1. Consider your goals.** Spend 2–3 hours reflecting on these, and bring your
answers to your first mentor meeting:

- What are your long-term career goals (academia, industry, government)?
- What professional-development opportunities are you hoping for?
- What are your research goals?
- What technical and non-technical skills would you like to develop?
- What steps will you take to get there?
- What are you most looking forward to?

You don't need perfect answers — this is a starting point for conversation.

**2. Draft your SMART goals.** Set goals each quarter using
[Develop quarterly SMART goals]({{ '/lab-guide/develop-smart-goals/' | relative_url }}); review them in your 1:1s.

**3. Present in a lab meeting.** Pick one paper from ~3 options your mentor
provides, read it, and prepare a strict 5-minute presentation: the big picture,
one or two key figures, and one question you still have. Your first slide should
introduce who you are, why you joined, and what you hope to learn.
