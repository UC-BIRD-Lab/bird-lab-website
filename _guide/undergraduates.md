## At a glance

<div class="guide-glance">
<div><span class="k">Time commitment</span>We request around 8 hours/week as a volunteer, growing if you enroll in EME/EAE 199 or want to take on more, agreed together and only as long as it benefits you. We understand you're volunteering your time and are grateful for it.</div>
<div><span class="k">What you'll do</span>Help with data collection and processing, basic analyses, and/or literature review, guided by a grad student or postdoc.</div>
<div><span class="k">What to expect from us</span>A defined mentor, regular check-ins, and support learning tools and research skills.</div>
<div><span class="k">What we expect from you</span>Show up reliably, communicate clearly, ask questions, and keep your work organized and backed up.</div>
</div>

Choosing how to take part? Undergraduates can volunteer or enroll for EME/EAE 199 credit; see [Join the Lab]({{ '/join/' | relative_url }}) for what each path involves.
{: .guide-info}

## Orientation & access <span class="when">Week 1</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Skim the lab guide.**

- Skim the [Lab Guide]({{ '/lab-guide/' | relative_url }}) to see what's where; return to pages as you need them.
{: .checklist}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**2. Complete the Slack onboarding workflow.**

- Ask Christina or your mentor to add you to the BIRD Lab Slack.
- Complete the onboarding workflow; it collects your info and the access you need, so you don't email anyone separately.
- *(If you're not enrolled at UC Davis)* fill out the Volunteer Form and return it to Christina.
- For project-specific computer access, follow the Request Access canvas in Slack.
{: .checklist}

New undergrads: check with Christina or your mentor before starting the onboarding workflow. Onboarding questions go to the onboarding officer (see [Lab operations roles]({{ '/lab-guide/lab-operations-roles/' | relative_url }})).
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**3. Complete safety training.**

- After the workflow, you'll be added to the **#safety** Slack channel; complete the safety workflows there.
- See [Lab safety]({{ '/lab-guide/lab-safety/' | relative_url }}) for the full list of trainings.
{: .checklist}

Undergrads are not given office keys and are not to be in the facilities alone.
{: .guide-info}

If your project involves animals, complete the required training early; see [Animal care & ethics]({{ '/lab-guide/animal-care-ethics/' | relative_url }}).
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**4. Install core tools and set up data backups.**

- Set up [UC Davis Microsoft 365](https://iet.ucdavis.edu/content/free-microsoft-office-365-now-available-all-uc-davis-students), a synced Google Calendar, and the Box Desktop App.
- Set up your backups and version control as described in [Data management]({{ '/lab-guide/data-management/' | relative_url }}).
{: .checklist}

See [Data management]({{ '/lab-guide/data-management/' | relative_url }}) for the full backup policy and [Code]({{ '/lab-guide/code/' | relative_url }}) for our Git practices.
{: .guide-info}

Recommended (ask your mentor which you'll use this quarter):

- [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/) and its [Word plugin](https://www.zotero.org/support/word_processor_plugin_usage).
- [Git](https://github.com/git-guides/install-git) and a GitHub account; if you're new, do [Introduction to GitHub](https://github.com/skills/introduction-to-github).
- The programming environment your project uses: [R & RStudio](https://rstudio-education.github.io/hopr/starting.html) and/or [Python & VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/).
{: .checklist}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**5. Set up mentor check-ins.** Arrange regular check-ins with your day-to-day
mentor (a grad student or postdoc), and agree the cadence together. See
[Meetings]({{ '/lab-guide/meetings/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

## Goals & mentoring <span class="when">Weeks 1–2</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Consider your goals.** Spend some time reflecting (maybe over a coffee or
tea), then set your quarterly goals; see [Goal setting]({{ '/lab-guide/goal-setting/' | relative_url }}).
Review them with your mentor.
</div>

## Research & growth <span class="when">Ongoing</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Present in a lab meeting.**

- Pick one paper from ~3 options your mentor provides.
- Read it and note what you understood and what confused you.
- Prepare a strict 5-minute talk: the big picture, one or two key figures, and one question you still have.
- On your first slide, introduce who you are (name, year, major), why you joined, and what you hope to learn.
{: .checklist}

Your mentor can help you design the slides and practice; see [Presentations]({{ '/lab-guide/presentations/' | relative_url }}) for tips.
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**2. Settle in.**

- Understand what's expected of your time; see [Work expectations]({{ '/lab-guide/work-expectations/' | relative_url }}).
- Find help when you need it; see [Support resources]({{ '/lab-guide/support-resources/' | relative_url }}).
- Wrapping up at the end of a quarter or your time here? See [Leaving the lab]({{ '/lab-guide/leaving-the-lab/' | relative_url }}).
{: .checklist}
</div>
