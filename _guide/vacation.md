You are welcome and encouraged to take vacation when and where you need it. If
you aren't taking any time off, you may be encouraged to at least take a
stay-cation to reset. BIRD Lab members are strongly encouraged to take a
**minimum of 15 vacation days per calendar year** — most members take 3–4 weeks.

The main ask: **log your time off** so people know when you're unavailable.

## How to request time off

We track vacation and leave with the **Vacation Tracker** app in Slack.

1. In Slack, type `/vacation`
2. Select your leave type, start date, and end date (reason optional)
3. Click **Send request**
4. Christina approves it in Slack, and it's stored automatically for reference

The first time you submit a request, click **Enable** when prompted so the app
can automatically set and clear your "Out of Office" Slack status.

## Sick days

If you're sick, please stay home and focus on recovering.

- If you feel up to light work, optional low-lift tasks include reading papers,
  writing, or planning.
- If not, fully log off — that's okay.

Please log sick time through `/vacation` (specify **Sick**) so others know you're out.

## Notice guidelines

These are guidelines, not bureaucracy — the goal is to reduce surprises and keep
projects moving.

| Length of leave | Suggested notice | Before you go |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Under 1 week | At least 1 day in advance | — |
| 1–2 weeks | About 1 month in advance | Quick handoff: who to contact, time-sensitive tasks needing coverage, and set an out-of-office email reply |
