What we expect around time, pay, and managing your projects, and what you can
expect from the lab in return.

## Work hours


<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
Refer to [Communicating in the lab]({{ '/lab-guide/communication/' | relative_url }}) for day-to-day communication policies.
</aside>

| Role | Time commitment |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Part-time (we request ~8 hrs/week as a volunteer; more with EME/EAE 199 units or by interest, as agreed) |
| Graduate (MSc & PhD) | Full-time (~40 hrs/week, incl. coursework) |
| Postdoc | Full-time (40 hrs/week) |

Hours vary week to week with classes, experiments, conferences, and deadlines.
A graduate researcher's load also shifts over time, with more coursework early
and more research later. 

Undergraduates should prioritize their GPA, and tell Christina if you need to adjust a given week.
Research should be a beneficial add-on not a substitution for courses. We request your time
rather than require it; we understand you're volunteering it, and we're grateful for every
hour you give.

<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
**Unsure of your workload?** Try a time audit for two to three weeks. Online
tools like [Toggl Track](https://toggl.com/track/) help.
</aside>

**For everyone:**

- **Flexible hours.** No fixed expectations about when or how long you're in the
  office, as long as the work gets done and your project is progressing.
- **Progress over hours.** We judge progress by the quality and consistency of
  your work and your growth, not hours in the office.
- **No weekend work expected.** See [Time off & vacation]({{ '/lab-guide/time-off-vacation/' | relative_url }})
  for leave policies.
- **Do not message after-hours.** You can work whenever you want, but don't expect the same from others. See [Communication]({{ '/lab-guide/communication/' | relative_url }}).
- **Unwell? Rest.** If you're not well, emotionally or physically, prioritize
  your health.

## Compensation

We're transparent about funding. Each fall, Christina gives a State of the Lab
update on the lab's funding, major initiatives, and plans for the year ahead.
Ask anytime about your appointment, support, or future funding. All salaries are overseen by MAE financial officers and follow UC Davis union agreements.

<aside class="marginnote" markdown="1">
**PhD funding goal.** Christina aims to support PhD researchers at 50% during the
academic year (the maximum permitted) and 50% over summer, with tuition and fees
covered, from grants, fellowships, TAships, and other sources. A goal, not a
guarantee; it depends on available funding.
</aside>

| Role | Funding |
|---|---|
| Undergraduate | Not guaranteed; possible after a one-quarter trial when funds allow |
| MSc | Not guaranteed; depends on available support |
| PhD | Paid |
| Postdoc | Paid |

All researchers are encouraged to apply for internal and external research
funding to support their own professional development.

## Project management

- **Manage your own project.** You should set and meet internal and external targets.
  Mentoring support helps you build those skills.
- **Plan intentionally.** We use Gantt charts, Kanban boards, and similar tools.
- **Post a weekly Slack update.** A few bullet points on your accomplishments, blockers, priorities, deadlines, and requests for support. This keeps Christina informed, surfaces issues early, and lets you look back at how far you've come.

## Deadlines & planning

- **Set and respect internal deadlines.** They prevent last-minute crunches and
  leave time for thoughtful feedback, careful revisions, and better science.
- **Flag slipping timelines early.** Small adjustments made early are far easier
  than emergency fixes near a deadline.
- **Christina plans weeks to months ahead** and aims to finish at least a week
  before deadlines, and appreciates the same notice in return.
- **The goal is sustainable progress, not constant urgency.**
