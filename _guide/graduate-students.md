## Foundation year <span class="when">Year 1</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Begin your literature review.** This is your core intellectual foundation;
see [Literature reviews]({{ '/lab-guide/literature-reviews/' | relative_url }}).

- Start a systematic literature review in your topic area.
- Organize papers and notes in Zotero.
{: .checklist}

Aim to build a living library you update throughout your first year and beyond.
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**2. Create a long-term plan.** By the end of your first year:

- Build a Gantt chart or visual timeline (coursework, preliminary exam, research milestones, paper submissions, conferences, thesis goals).
- Share and refine it in your 1:1s.
- (Recommended) Set up a Kanban board (Backlog / This month / This week / In progress / Done) linked to your milestones.
{: .checklist}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**3. Select your course schedule.** Courses should support your research and skill growth.

- Draft a course plan aligned with your project and timeline.
- Discuss it with Christina; registration specifics (290C and 299) and current course suggestions are in the [member portal]({{ '/portal/' | relative_url }}).
- Browse the UC Davis [expanded course descriptions](https://statistics.ucdavis.edu/courses/expanded-descriptions).
- Take at least one statistics course (e.g., EVE 225; more options in the member portal).
- Take a bird course (e.g., WFC 111).
{: .checklist}

Program requirements: [MAE doctoral degree](https://mae.ucdavis.edu/graduate/doctoral-degree) and [MAE master's degree](https://mae.ucdavis.edu/graduate/master-of-science).
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**4. Pass the preliminary exam.** PhD students take the MAE Preliminary Examination
at the first opportunity; it is offered once a year, in Spring quarter. Students who
have passed the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam, Mechanical discipline, are
exempt; submit proof of passing to the Graduate Program before the first offering.
The detailed prep guide is in the [member portal]({{ '/portal/' | relative_url }}).

- Confirm your exam date, or submit FE proof if you are exempt.
{: .checklist}

Exempt via the [FE-Mechanical exam](https://ncees.org/exams/fe-exam/)? Submit proof to the Graduate Program before the first offering of the preliminary exam.
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**5. Apply for fellowships early.** Major fellowship deadlines (e.g., NSF GRFP) fall
early in year 1; see [Funding applications]({{ '/lab-guide/funding-applications/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

## Research & growth <span class="when">Ongoing</span>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**1. Present your research.** Graduate students present at lab meetings about twice
a year; see [Presentations]({{ '/lab-guide/presentations/' | relative_url }}) and
[Meetings]({{ '/lab-guide/meetings/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**2. Complete your annual progress review.** Every year, UC Davis runs the
[Student Progress Assessment (SPA)](https://grad.ucdavis.edu/student-progress-assessment)
for PhD and master's students.

- Review the SPA questions and draft your own answers first.
- Christina sets up individual one-hour one-on-ones in June focused on this, in addition to your regular weekly meetings.
{: .checklist}

Coming in with your own draft makes the review yours; it becomes a conversation about your development, not a form to fill in.
{: .guide-info}
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**3. Keep developing.** Revisit your development plan and career direction as you
progress; see [Goal setting]({{ '/lab-guide/goal-setting/' | relative_url }})
and [Career planning & job search]({{ '/lab-guide/career-planning/' | relative_url }}).
</div>

<div class="guide-step" markdown="1">
**4. Settle in.**

- Understand what's expected of your time; see [Work expectations]({{ '/lab-guide/work-expectations/' | relative_url }}).
- Find help when you need it; see [Support resources]({{ '/lab-guide/support-resources/' | relative_url }}).
- Wrapping up a chapter or leaving? See [Leaving the lab]({{ '/lab-guide/leaving-the-lab/' | relative_url }}).
{: .checklist}
</div>
