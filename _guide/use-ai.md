Large language models are changing how we do science. We move with the times
while developing as scientists and engineers and holding to high, reproducible
standards. As a lab, we agree to the following.

<div class="callout callout--stop" markdown="1">
**Protect sensitive information.** Do not use generative-AI tools with sensitive
UC Davis systems or data. Never provide inputs that might contain student
information, personnel data, patient data, confidential financial or research
information, or otherwise sensitive university information.
</div>

## Appropriate uses

- Code debugging and coding assistance (e.g., Copilot).
- Improving the code that generates R or Python plots.
- Editing existing drafts for grammar and sentence structure.
- Brainstorming ideas — as long as you don't limit brainstorming to AI alone.

## Inappropriate uses

- Initial writing drafts.
- Believing what AI tells you without double-checking and doing your due diligence.
- Generating images for publication.
- Uploading any data produced or collected in our lab or others'.
- Fabricating or falsifying data.
- Sharing any personnel or student information.
- Presenting AI content as your own — all use must be acknowledged, and AI is never an author.
- Avoiding learning or problem-solving in favor of trusting AI; that defeats the purpose of research.

> *AI golden rule: use AI with others as you would want them to use AI with you.*
> — [Stanford Report](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2025/01/report-outlines-stanford-principles-for-use-of-ai)

## When you do use AI

**Acknowledge it.** If a generative tool is used, your publications should
acknowledge which tool was used and how — even if no AI-generated content was
incorporated.

**Be aware of limitations.** AI can reflect bias and stereotypes, produce fake
citations, and perform calculations incorrectly. Always verify against tools
designed for the task.

**Follow the [UC Davis Code of Academic Conduct](https://ossja.ucdavis.edu/code-academic-conduct).**
Review and verify any AI-generated content you publish, and consider copyright —
you are responsible for anything you publish. Never copy-paste AI language or use
AI to generate images for publications.
