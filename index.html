A signature idea

How bio-inspired is your design?

Inspiration can be nebulous. We identified three key components for transparent bio-inspired research reporting and hold our own work to the same standard.

Harvey, C. (2026). “How bio-inspired is your design? A transparent reporting framework.” Communications Engineering. doi:10.1038/s44172-026-00641-4

1

Source of inspiration

Explicit identification of the species or family from which the idea originates.

2

Level of mimicry

How closely the design mimics biology versus abstracting a principle from it.

3

Strength of evidence

How well the implemented method is supported by scientific evidence.

Open science

We run our lab in the open

Transparency is part of the science. Our day-to-day handbook is public, our data and code go public on publication, and we write plain-language summaries so anyone can follow the work.

Public Lab Guide

A searchable, living-document wiki of how we run the lab open for anyone to read.

Code and Datasets
