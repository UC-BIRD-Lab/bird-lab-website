## The short version

We study birds to find the principles behind flight, then test which of those
principles actually earn a place in engineered systems. You might measure a real
raptor in flight at our [CALI flight hall]({{ '/facilities/' | relative_url }}) and
model its aerodynamics the same week. **You do not need training in both biology
and engineering** — most people arrive strong in one and pick up the other here.

## What to expect

- **Your own project**, from question to publication, with weekly mentoring — not a task list.
- **A broad toolkit:** wind tunnels, motion capture, CFD, flight-dynamics modeling, machine learning, and live-animal work.
- **Real output:** conference travel, co-authored papers, and open data and code you can point to.
- **A lab that writes down how it works** — see the public [Lab Guide]({{ '/lab-guide/' | relative_url }}) — and takes safety, inclusion, and honest science seriously.

## Openings right now

**Undergraduates (UC Davis)** — We interview one or two students most quarters;
all majors welcome. Strong researchers can be paid after a trial quarter. Fastest
way in: the [interest form]({{ site.undergrad_form_url }}).

**Graduate students** — No funded openings at the moment, but if you can bring or
pursue a fellowship, get in touch. Christina advises in both
[Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering](https://mae.ucdavis.edu/graduate) (MS/PhD) and
[Animal Behavior](https://anb.ucdavis.edu/) (PhD). Interviews start in **early
fall**, ahead of UC Davis's
[December deadlines](https://grad.ucdavis.edu/program-application-deadlines) — reach
out then to be considered for the lab specifically.

**Postdocs** — No funded openings now; if you're interested, email and we can look
at fellowship options together.

## How to reach out

- **UC Davis undergrads** → the [interest form]({{ site.undergrad_form_url }}).
- **Everyone else** → email Christina
  (<a href="mailto:{{ site.lab.pi_email }}">{{ site.lab.pi_email }}</a>) with a CV,
  transcript, and a few lines on what you'd like to work on.

A good first email is four or five lines: who you are, why *this* lab (name a
[project]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}) or [paper]({{ '/publications/' | relative_url }})
that caught you), what you want to learn, and your timing. Attach a CV and
transcript. She aims to reply within two weeks — nudge her if she doesn't. Address
it to "Dr. Harvey" to start; first names once you're in.
